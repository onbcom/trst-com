## How It Works (The Trust Stack)
1. **Verification**: Confirming the identity of entities (human or AI) using cryptographic and biometric proofs.
2. **Authentication**: The continuous process of ensuring access rights remain valid and secure without compromising privacy.
3. **Reputation**: Aggregating historical signals to predict future reliability within a network.
4. **Enforcement**: Utilizing smart contracts and automated compliance to ensure trust isn't just promised, but architected.

## FAQ Section
1. **Why is digital trust more important in 2026?**
With the explosion of generative AI and autonomous agents, the "cost of deception" has dropped to near zero. Robust trust systems are the only way to distinguish between genuine entities and malicious synthetics.
2. **What is a "Trust Layer"?**
A trust layer is a software and protocol stack that sits between the network and application layers, specifically handling identity, permissioning, and integrity.
3. **How does TRST evaluate technologies?**
We assess platforms based on four pillars: cryptographic security, user privacy, scalability, and interoperability across different digital ecosystems.
4. **Is TRST a security platform?**
No. Security is about keeping people out; Trust is about letting the right people in and ensuring they can transact with confidence.
5. **What role does Decentralized Identity (DID) play?**
DID is the foundation of digital trust, removing the need for centralized silos and giving users a portable, verifiable digital "passport" they control.
